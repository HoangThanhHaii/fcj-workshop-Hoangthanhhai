# Summary Report on “EVENT 9/5”

### Purpose of the Event

- The event was organized to inspire and equip students and young developers with a comprehensive toolkit of mindset, practical tech skills, and career guidance in the digital era.

### List of Speakers

- Hoang Long (AWS Research Group) – Shared about learning motivation and Dopamine.
- Nguyen Khang – Presented on Optimal Prompt Engineering Techniques.
- Solution Architect from Cloud Kinetics – Career orientation and general mindset.
- Nguyen Thao Phuong – Software Developer at VIB, presented the BMX method.

### Event Highlights
- Psychological motivation exploring the mechanism of Dopamine release in the brain and behavioral psychology, helping developers ignite inspiration, maintain long-term focus, and turn learning technology into a sustainable joy.
- Mastering AI with Optimal Prompt Engineering.
- Enterprise-Standard Working Methods.
- Career Orientation and Cloud Architecture Mindset.

### What Was Learned
- Regarding personal mindset:
  + Understood the relationship between Dopamine and focus, thereby learning how to set up a "reward loop" to keep learning technology engaging and maintain long-term perseverance.
  + AI mastery skills: Learned how to communicate, give precise prompts, and optimize AI to assist in writing code, debugging, looking up documentation, and automating repetitive tasks.
  + Practical perspective from enterprises: Understood what standards large corporations/banks require from a Software Developer in a real-world working environment.

### Application to Work
- Activate motivation & work performance (From insights on Dopamine).
- Know how to control distractions to keep the brain in a state of deep work.
- Apply standard prompt techniques to use AI for writing unit tests, generating mock data, writing documentation, or converting data formats (JSON, SQL) in seconds.
- Integrate the BMX working method into personal or team workflow management, enhancing discipline, tracking progress effectively, and ensuring high-quality outputs.

### Event Experience

Participating in the May 9 event was a very rewarding experience. Through the session on Dopamine, you will understand why you often lose focus or feel unmotivated when studying/working in technology, while learning how to "hack" your psychology to rediscover your passion for every line of code. The learning spirit is heightened when seeing fresh, relatable perspectives from both young speakers and seasoned experts. Listening to the Solution Architect from Cloud Kinetics feels like a 1-on-1 Q&A session, helping you clearly visualize the panoramic picture of the Cloud industry and understand what preparations are needed for your next career step.

#### Lessons Learned
- Motivation comes from Dopamine management, not force: Studying and working in the IT industry is a long journey. The lesson learned is to break down goals to create continuous "small wins," helping the natural release of Dopamine in the brain, maintaining joy and sustainable perseverance without burnout.
- Do not rely too heavily on AI; AI is only as powerful as the user's ability to ask the right questions. AI does not replace humans, but those who know how to use AI effectively will replace those who are overly dependent on it.
